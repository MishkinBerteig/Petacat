## Relationship to the original Metacat

The original Metacat (James B. Marshall, 1999) was distributed under the
**GNU General Public License version 2**. The canonical distribution lives
at <http://science.slc.edu/~jmarshall/metacat/>. The architectural ideas —
Workspace, Slipnet, Coderack, Themespace, Temporal Trace, Episodic Memory,
Temperature, jootsing, and the rest — are Dr. Marshall's, building on
earlier Copycat work by Melanie Mitchell and Douglas Hofstadter.

**Petacat is a port, not a clean-room reimplementation.** It was written
using *both* Dr. Marshall's published 1999 PhD dissertation (*Metacat: A
Self-Watching Cognitive Architecture for Analogy-Making and High-Level
Perception*, Indiana University) *and* the original Scheme source code as
references. Large portions of the Python code were developed alongside the
corresponding Scheme files, with structure, naming, and flow often following
the original closely. Because the work therefore derives from GPL-2 licensed
code, the port would by default be covered by the GPL-2 and could not be
released under a more permissive license unilaterally.

**Dr. Marshall has, however, granted express written permission** for
Petacat to be released under an MIT-style license. He did so by email in
April 2026 in response to a direct request from the Petacat author. That
permission is what makes the MIT license below legally valid for this
project; without it, Petacat would necessarily have been released under
GPL-2-or-later to match the original.

questionable design choices in the port are the responsibility of the
Petacat author, not of Dr. Marshall or the authors of Copycat.
